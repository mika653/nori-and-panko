The Complete Puppy Vaccine Schedule in the Philippines (2025)

When should your puppy get their first shot? What vaccines are required vs. optional? We break it all down so your pup gets the protection they need, when they need it.

🐶
Dr. Nori
Lead Veterinarian, Feline & Canine Specialist

Getting a puppy is one of the most exciting things in the world. But between choosing the right food, puppy-proofing your home, and figuring out bathroom training, vaccination schedules can feel overwhelming.

Here's the good news: it doesn't have to be complicated. As home service vets who visit puppies all over Metro Manila, we've put together this guide to explain exactly what your puppy needs, when, and why.

Note from our team: All puppies are different. This guide covers the standard vaccination protocol for the Philippines, but our vet will always tailor recommendations based on your puppy's age, health status, and risk factors during the home visit.

Why Puppy Vaccines Matter

Puppies are born with some immunity from their mother's milk (called maternal antibodies), but this protection fades quickly — usually by 8 to 16 weeks of age. Once that window closes, your puppy is vulnerable to serious, potentially fatal diseases.

Vaccines work by training your puppy's immune system to recognize and fight specific viruses and bacteria before they ever encounter the real thing. The diseases they protect against — like Parvovirus and Distemper — are highly contagious and often fatal in unvaccinated dogs.

The Standard Puppy Vaccine Schedule

The following is the recommended schedule for puppies in the Philippines, based on Philippine Veterinary Medical Association (PVMA) guidelines and Bureau of Animal Industry (BAI) standards:

Age Vaccines Notes
6 weeks 5-in-1 (DHPPiL) — 1st dose Optional if dam was vaccinated; recommended for high-risk environments
8–9 weeks 5-in-1 (DHPPiL) — 2nd dose Core vaccine. Required for most groomers and boarding facilities
12 weeks 5-in-1 (DHPPiL) — 3rd dose + Rabies 1st dose Rabies vaccine is legally required for dogs 3 months and older in the Philippines
16 weeks 5-in-1 (DHPPiL) — 4th dose + Bordetella (optional) Bordetella recommended if your dog visits parks or other dogs regularly
6 months Rabies booster Second Rabies dose to complete primary series
1 year Annual 5-in-1 booster + Annual Rabies booster Annual boosters maintain protection throughout your dog's life

What is the 5-in-1 (DHPPiL)?

The 5-in-1 or DHPPiL is a combination vaccine that protects against five diseases in a single injection:

  • D — Distemper: A serious viral illness affecting the respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems
  • H — Hepatitis (Adenovirus): Causes liver disease and respiratory illness
  • P — Parvovirus: Highly contagious; can be fatal in puppies without immediate treatment
  • Pi — Parainfluenza: A respiratory virus contributing to Kennel Cough
  • L — Leptospirosis: A bacterial disease spread through contaminated water or urine — very common in the Philippines
Tip: Leptospirosis is particularly common in the Philippines because of flooding and urban rodent populations. Don't skip the "L" in DHPPiL, even if your dog doesn't go outdoors often.

Rabies Vaccination in the Philippines

Rabies is legally required for all dogs 3 months of age and older under the Philippine Rabies Act of 2007 (Republic Act 9482). Unvaccinated dogs may not be licensed with your local government unit (LGU).

Anti-rabies vaccines are available during our home visits. We provide an official Rabies Vaccination Certificate signed by our licensed veterinarian, which is accepted by LGUs for dog licensing.

Optional (Non-Core) Vaccines

These vaccines are not required for all dogs but may be recommended depending on your dog's lifestyle:

  • Bordetella (Kennel Cough): Recommended if your dog visits parks, boarding facilities, or dog shows
  • Lyme Disease: Rarely recommended in the Philippines but available on request
  • Canine Influenza: For dogs with high exposure to other dogs

Why Home Vaccination Makes Sense

Bringing a puppy to a veterinary clinic — especially for their first few vaccinations — comes with a real risk. Puppies who haven't completed their vaccine series are vulnerable to diseases they might encounter in a waiting room.

Common Questions

Can I take my puppy outside before finishing all vaccines?

We recommend avoiding dog parks, pet stores, and places where unknown dogs roam until your puppy has completed at least their 3rd DHPPiL at 12 weeks. Your own garden or a clean home environment is fine for socialization in the meantime.

My puppy is older than 16 weeks — is it too late to start?

No, it is never too late. Older puppies and adult dogs who have never been vaccinated can start at any age. The schedule is adjusted to their current age. Book a visit and our vet will assess and design the right protocol.

What side effects should I expect after vaccination?

Mild side effects are normal and usually resolve within 24–48 hours:

  • Slight lethargy or reduced appetite
  • Small lump at the injection site (resolves on its own)
  • Low-grade fever

If your puppy shows facial swelling, difficulty breathing, vomiting, or collapse within an hour of vaccination, contact us or go to an emergency clinic immediately — though this is very rare.
