Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our home service veterinary visits — how it works, what to expect, pricing, and more.

🛠 How It Works

It's simple. You book online through our website in five easy steps: enter your pet's info, choose a service, pick a date and time, provide your contact details, and pay the ₱500 consultation fee.

On the day of your visit, our licensed vet team arrives at your home fully equipped. We handle everything on-site — examination, vaccination, treatment, or procedure. No travel stress for your pet, no clinic waiting rooms for you.

We currently cover most of Metro Manila, including:

  • Quezon City, Marikina, San Juan, Mandaluyong
  • Makati, Taguig, Pasig, Pateros
  • Muntinlupa, Las Piñas, Parañaque, Caloocan

We're actively expanding. If you're unsure whether we serve your specific barangay, just message us on Facebook and we'll confirm quickly.

Our team arrives fully prepared for the service booked. For vaccinations, we bring licensed, refrigerated vaccines and syringes. For surgical procedures like spay/neuter (kapon), we bring sterile surgical instruments, anesthesia, and monitoring equipment.

We ask clients to prepare a clean, flat surface (a table or floor space works fine). For surgical procedures, we'll share specific preparation guidelines after booking confirmation.

It depends on the service:

  • Vaccination: 20–40 minutes
  • Wellness Consultation: 30–60 minutes
  • Deworming: 15–30 minutes
  • Grooming: 1–3 hours depending on pet size and coat
  • Spay/Neuter (Kapon): 2–4 hours including preparation and monitoring
  • Senior Pet Exam: 45–75 minutes

💉 Vaccinations

We offer a full range of canine vaccines, including:

  • Core vaccines: 5-in-1 (DHPPiL), Rabies
  • Puppy series: 6 weeks, 9 weeks, 12 weeks protocol
  • Non-core: Bordetella (Kennel Cough), Leptospirosis booster

All vaccines are licensed by the Bureau of Animal Industry (BAI) and stored properly in cold chain transport.

For cats, we provide:

  • Core vaccines: Feline 3-in-1 (FVRCP — panleukopenia, herpesvirus, calicivirus), Rabies
  • Kitten series: Starting at 8 weeks, with boosters at 12 and 16 weeks
  • FeLV: Feline Leukemia Virus vaccine (upon assessment)

Yes. After every vaccination, our vet will issue an official vaccination certificate signed and stamped by the attending veterinarian. This document is accepted by pet groomers, boarding facilities, and government offices for licensing.

Digital copies can also be sent via Facebook Messenger upon request.

Puppies can start their vaccine series as early as 6 weeks old. Kittens typically begin at 8 weeks. However, maternal antibodies from the mother can interfere with early vaccines, so timing matters.

Our vet team will assess your pet's exact age and health status to recommend the ideal starting point and schedule.

✂️ Spay & Neuter (Kapon)

Yes — when performed by licensed veterinarians with proper equipment, home kapon is safe and increasingly common. Our team brings all surgical instruments, anesthesia agents, monitoring devices, and emergency supplies.

We conduct a pre-op assessment before every procedure. If our vet team determines during the assessment that the procedure should be done in a clinical setting (due to health complications or risk factors), we will advise accordingly and not proceed at home.

After your booking is confirmed, we'll send you a full pre-op preparation guide. In general:

  • No food for 8–12 hours before the procedure (water is allowed up to 2 hours before)
  • Keep your pet calm and confined the night before
  • Prepare a clean, flat space with a clean sheet or mat
  • Have someone home to assist during the procedure
  • No baths 3 days before

Most pets recover well within 7–14 days. After the procedure:

  • Your pet will be groggy from anesthesia for several hours — this is normal
  • Keep them in a quiet, warm, clean space
  • No baths for 10–14 days; keep the wound dry
  • Administer medications as prescribed by our vet
  • Check the wound daily — redness or swelling that increases should be reported to us immediately

We follow up with a free virtual check-in within 48 hours via Facebook Messenger to monitor recovery.

The ideal age varies by species and breed:

  • Dogs: Generally 6 months and above. Large breeds may benefit from waiting until 12–18 months. Our vet will assess.
  • Cats: 4–6 months is typically recommended. Earlier spay/neuter in cats is common and safe.

We perform a health assessment before any procedure. Pets with fever, infection, pregnancy, or other active health issues will be advised to wait.

✂️ Grooming

Our home grooming services include:

  • Bath & Blow Dry: Medicated or anti-flea shampoo, conditioner, blow dry, brushing
  • Haircut & Styling: Breed-specific cut or custom trim based on your preference
  • Nail Trim: Safe nail clipping for dogs and cats
  • Ear Cleaning: Gentle ear inspection and cleaning

All grooming is done by our trained groomer who is supervised by our vet team for pet safety.

Just a few simple things:

  • A water source (outdoor faucet, shower, or bathtub) accessible to the groomer
  • A clean area — outdoors or a bathroom works well
  • Towels that can get wet and fur on them
  • Your pet's preferred shampoo if you have a brand preference (we also bring our own medicated options)
